About Tim

I’m an dataviz and multimedia designer from Washington, D.C. Currently, I am the design and visuals manager at the Urban Institute, a non-partisan social and economic research institution. At Urban, I lead a team of designers and developers that distill complex information into high-impact, visually digestible presentations. My team and I have lead the intstitute into a new age of visual storytelling, while updating the organizations visual brand identity as well as the web site.

Previous to my time at Urban, I was the Creative Director at the NEED Project, a small non-profit that works with K-12 students across the country, teaching them about energy — everything ranging from wind and natural gas to the physics of light, sound and motion.

Before moving to Washington, I was an Infographics Artist at The Columbus Dispatch, a major metropolitan newspaper and central Ohio’s most popular web site. I started at The Dispatch in 2007 as I was finishing my Master’s degree in Photography at Ohio University’s School of Visual Communication. My master’s project explored the impact of multimedia on the design of national news web sites during the 2008 presidential election.

My backgrounds in computer science, art, design, and journalism have provided me with a unique set of skills that keep me on the cutting edge of new media technology and visual communication. I’m very quick to adapt to any new challange and I’m a voracious learner. I am detail oriented, a clear and concise editor, and cool under deadline.

I’m happily married to my wife, Leslie, a Veterinarian practicing in Arlington, Virginia. We recently had our first child, Eleanor, who keeps us on our toes. We’ve got two dogs, Bonnie and Vernon, and two cats, Trudie and Charlie. I love to cook anything that takes more than six hours to make: I’ve won awards for my chili recipe, I’m an avid backyard barbecue enthusiast, and I brew my own beer.

What you can expect when working with me

A good designer will push and pull. As a client, you bring a designer in because you need somebody with a specialized set of skills—somebody who can help you translate your work to your intended audiences. A good designer knows to ask questions and will make sure that they understand the content before trying to communicate it to somebody else. A good designer is a partner, not a service provider. The greatest skill I have is my ability to solve problems. Projects succeed when clients come to me and say "We have this great work and we want to showcase it with an illustration. We like your work, knock yourself out." Or "I've got this data set that shows some interesting trends. How should we bring it to life?" I love it when a client understands that they're good at what they do, and that I'm good at what I do. That's how we make magic.
